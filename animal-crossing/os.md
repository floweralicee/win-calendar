# Operating System — Alice Chen

name: Alice Chen
tagline: Animator → Builder → Creator

## Core: Clarity-driven, not Discipline-driven

She doesn't move when the map is gone. The moment clarity appears, she can't stop.
Most productivity systems are built for discipline-type people. This one is built for her.

## Daily startup sequence

1. Night before: decide tomorrow's small win (5-15 min, directly connected to main goal)
2. Morning: do that small win before touching phone
3. Look at yesterday's DONE list — 2 minutes
4. Write today's ONE THING — just one
5. Optional: send one message to anyone, let the world know you're moving

## Peak window

7–11am: sharpest brain, best for writing, decisions, planning
1–4pm: execution mode — emails, code, mechanical tasks
8pm+: creative mode — ideas, journaling, new directions (don't execute late-night pivots until morning)

## Fuel (what generates clarity)

- Seeing her own wins — looking at DONE list, data, proof she's moving
- Completing one small thing — any small thing
- Social feedback — someone responding to her content or messages

## Warning signs she's avoiding something

- Suddenly wants to build a new tool or pivot direction (especially at night)
- Spending hours designing a system instead of using it
- Asking "what should I do today" instead of just doing
- Turning a 30-minute task into something that needs to be "thought through"

## Energy drains

- Relationship conflict — system's biggest single-point failure
- No map / no clarity — everything stalls
- Too many options at once — decision fatigue stacks fast
- Doing work with no feedback loop — disappears into silence
